Overview:
July 4th may have been yesterday, but local Independece Day celebrations are continuing into this weekend. From fireworks in Windham to the parade in Phoneicia, this weekend will provide plenty of opportunities to get outside and enjoy our Hudson Valley communities. That being said, there will be active weather this weekend that should be noted as you make your personal plans. Hot temperatures and possible storms will impact Saturday, so continue reading the latest forecast so you can plan accordingly.

As of 3PM, current radar reveals a weather system to our west, near Michigan. Radar, satellite, and analysis from the Weather Prediction Center currently indicate that the cold front associated with this weather system is triggering a sprawling arc of storms that currently extends from Michigan, into the southeastern states, and back across Texas. Over the next 24 hours, the cold front will approach our region. A southerly airflow ahead of the front will lead to increasing moisture on Saturday. This is evident through dewpoints that will increase to the mid-70s and precipital water values increasing to just over 2 inches, these values are higher than 99% of all July days. Both of these values are indicative of very moist, muggy air. Both computer modeling and extrapolation suggest that the moisture associated with the storm will begin to trigger rain tomorrow morning. The most likely timing for any precipitation will be between 4AM and 9AM. Steady rain is likely during this time, followed by a chance of scattered showers and thunderstorms throughout the afternoon. Rainfall totals of 0.25 to 0.50 inches are likley with isolated areas of up to 1.5 inches where stronger storms develop. The Impacts:
Rain and flooding.
With the chance of scattered heavier downpours tomorrow, there is a chance for areas of flooding. The Weather Prediction Center has highlighted eastern New York as having a marginal risk of excessive rainfall throughout the day tomorrow. This means that there is a low, but higher than normal, chance that heavy rainfall could result in areas of flash flooding. In their words, isolated flash flooding is possible. Any flash flooding is likely to be localized and primarily affect places that can experience rapid runoff with heavy rainfall.

Visit weather.gov/aly for the latests forecasts and hazard warnings.

Dangerous heat.
Warm temperatures will combine with moist air tomorrow to produce dangerous heat. The National Weather Center in Albany has issued a heat advisory for the mid-Hudson Valley. The heat advisory is in place from 1PM to 8PM Saturday for temperatures that may feel has hot as 97 degrees. The advisory means that hot temperatures and high humidity may cause heat illnesses.

Visit cdc.gov/extreme-heat/signs-symptoms/index.html to learn the symptoms of heat illness.
